Why Golf?

Golf isn't just a sport — it's a philosophy. You're playing against the course, against yourself, and against your last round, all at once. There are no teammates to bail you out and no referee to blame. Just you, the club, the lie, and the shot.

I got into golf because of the patience it demands. Every round is a masterclass in decision-making under pressure — do you lay up or go for it? Play safe or attack the pin? Sound familiar? Those are the same questions I want to be answering in boardrooms someday.

What Golf Has Taught Me

  1. Patience wins.
    • Bogeys happen — panic makes it worse
    • Play the long game, not the hero shot every time
    • One bad hole doesn't define a round
  2. Preparation is everything.
    • Study the course before you tee off
    • Know your distances, know your tendencies
    • The best shot starts before you swing
  3. Mental fortitude matters most.
    • Visualization before every shot
    • Reset quickly after mistakes
    • Confidence is a choice
  4. Every round is a chance to improve.
    • Track what went wrong and why
    • Celebrate small wins — a great chip, a made putt
    • Consistency over brilliance

Favorite Courses in Utah

Utah is criminally underrated golf territory. The backdrop of red rock, mountains, and desert makes every round feel cinematic.

Thanksgiving Point Golf Club

Johnny Miller-designed, 7,700+ yards of pure challenge in Lehi. The views of the Wasatch Front are unreal.

Entrada at Snow Canyon

Johnny Miller again. Built into the red rock of St. George, this course is visually stunning and demands precision off every tee.

Soldier Hollow Golf Course

Nestled in Midway with Wasatch Mountain views. Great for a slower round and big elevation changes that make club selection an adventure.

Quick Tips for Beginners

01

Grip pressure: light

Hold the club like you're holding a baby bird. Most beginners grip too tight, which kills clubhead speed and causes pulls.

02

Setup is everything

Feet shoulder-width, knees slightly bent, spine tilted forward. If your setup is wrong, the swing doesn't stand a chance.

03

Don't look up early

Eyes on the ball through impact. Most topped shots come from lifting your head to watch the ball — trust the swing.

04

Short game first

60–70% of your strokes happen inside 100 yards. Spend more time on chipping and putting than beating drivers on the range.
