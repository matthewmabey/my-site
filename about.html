About Matthew

Who I Am

I'm a BYU Pre-Business freshman from Highland, Utah — driven by curiosity, a love for strategy, and a genuine belief that the best work happens at the intersection of people and ideas.

My path has been anything but linear. I grew up working on my family's farm, spent summers at Zion National Park doing hands-on renovation projects, and launched a marketing agency before most people my age had landed their first job.

But the experience that shaped me most was two years as a volunteer in Mexico City. I learned to communicate across cultural barriers, lead teams with patience, and find common ground with anyone. I came back fluent in Spanish and fluent in people.

Where I'm Headed

I'm building toward a career in high-stakes finance — investment banking, venture capital, private equity, or real estate. I want to work on deals that matter, in rooms where strategy and capital collide.

I'm also fascinated by how industries intersect — particularly healthcare administration and finance, where complex systems meet human impact.

Long-term, I want to be the kind of person who builds things, backs good founders, and leaves places better than he found them.
